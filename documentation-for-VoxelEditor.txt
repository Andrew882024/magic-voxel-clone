VoxelEditor — Using Guide
========================

The VoxelEditor is a 3D voxel (block) editor. You build by placing and removing colored cubes on a grid. Add and remove only happen on a complete click (pointer down then pointer up) without dragging; if you drag past a small threshold, the action is cancelled and the camera may orbit or pan instead.

—————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————
ADDING VOXELS
—————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————

• On the floor
  Use a complete click (press and release without dragging) with the left button on the gray floor plane to place a voxel at that position (at ground level, y = 0). The cursor becomes a crosshair when over the floor. If Shift is held, no voxel is placed.

• On a block face (stacking)
  Use a complete click (press and release without dragging) with the left button on any face of an existing voxel to place a new voxel on the adjacent cell in that direction. Use this to build upward and in all directions. If Shift is held, the complete click is treated as remove (see below) instead of add.

The color of newly placed voxels is the one currently selected in the left sidebar.

—————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————
REMOVING VOXELS
—————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————

• Shift + complete click (press and release without dragging) with the left button on a voxel to remove it. This only works when the complete click is on an existing block, not on the floor.

—————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————
COLOR
—————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————

• Palette
  The left sidebar shows a grid of preset colors. Click a swatch to select it; the selected color has a white border. New voxels use the selected color.

• Custom color
  Use the "Custom" color picker below the palette to choose any color. That color becomes the current selection for placing voxels.

—————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————
CAMERA (3D VIEW)
—————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————

• Orbit — Drag with the left mouse button to rotate the camera around the scene.
• Pan — Hold Ctrl (or Cmd) and drag with the left mouse button to pan the view.
• Zoom — Scroll the mouse wheel to move the camera closer or farther (between 4 and 80 units).

—————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————
CLEAR ALL
—————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————

The "Clear all" button at the bottom of the left sidebar removes every voxel and resets the scene. There is no undo.

—————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————
INTERFACE LAYOUT
—————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————————

• Header — Title and short hint for main controls.
• Left sidebar — Color palette, custom color picker, and "Clear all".
• Main area — 3D canvas with grid, floor, and voxels. All editing and camera controls happen here.

The buildable area is a 32×32 grid on the floor; voxels can be stacked above it. The grid is infinite in appearance but placement is constrained to this region.
